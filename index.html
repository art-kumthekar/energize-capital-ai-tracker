Energize Capital operates at the intersection of capital and innovation, and that orientation extends to how we run our own firm. We approach AI adoption the same way we approach investing: with rigor, selectivity, and a clear view of where value is actually created. Rather than deploying tools broadly or reactively, we evaluate AI with discipline, targeting specific process components where the improvement in speed, accuracy, or output quality is demonstrable. The tools that earn a place in our workflow are the ones that make us sharper — better at sourcing, more precise in our analysis, and more effective at driving performance across our portfolio.

Step 1 — Map the current process
Define and document each component of the process under review. Who is involved? What inputs and outputs exist? Where do handoffs occur? Identify where friction, delays, or inconsistency most frequently arise.
▾
Step 2 — Baseline time and cost
Quantify the current cost to produce a single quality output or result. Measure in hours per output, labor cost per output, or error rate. Establish a clear, measurable benchmark before any tool is introduced.
▾
Step 3 — Assess the tool's marginal improvement
Identify precisely which part of the process the tool addresses. Estimate the new time and cost per output with the tool in place. Express the delta clearly: e.g., 4 hrs → 1 hr per output, or $200 → $60 per task.
▾
Step 4 — Determine scale of impact
How many team members would benefit from this tool? How frequently is this process run — daily, weekly, per deal? Multiply marginal gain by frequency and headcount to estimate total impact.
▾
Step 5 — Review external user feedback
What do verified users of this tool report about its real-world performance? Seek reviews from firms with similar workflows or deal volume. Prioritize substantive feedback over marketing claims or vendor case studies.
